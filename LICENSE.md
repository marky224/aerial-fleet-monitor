## What this is

Aerial Fleet Monitor (AFM) is a personal portfolio project. The source is public so that recruiters, hiring managers, and engineers can read it. Making the source viewable is **not** the same as making it free to use.

## What you can do

- **Read** the code on GitHub.
- **Clone** a local copy so you can read it more comfortably in your editor.

## What you can't do without asking first

- **Run it** in production, in staging, in a demo for your customers, or as part of any product or service.
- **Use any part of it** — code, schemas, infrastructure config, runbooks, prompts, docs — inside your own project or your employer's codebase.
- **Republish it** anywhere, in whole or in part, including forks intended for use rather than review.
- **Build on it** — derivative works, ports to another stack, translations, "inspired by" rewrites that copy non-trivial structure.
- **Reuse the name** "Aerial Fleet Monitor" or "AFM" for anything else.

If you want to do any of the above, email `me@markandrewmarquez.com` and ask. The default answer is "let's talk" — not "no."

## Why this license

AFM exists to demonstrate the author's engineering, architecture, and product judgment in public. A permissive license (MIT, Apache) would invite reuse the author isn't trying to support; a closed-source repo would defeat the portfolio purpose. Proprietary-but-source-available threads the needle: anyone can evaluate the work, but the author keeps the right to decide where the code goes next.
